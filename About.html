Why I started this journey

I started using Quora in the summer of 2017

I wanted to improve my writing so I thought what better way then to answer Quora questions.

At this time I had been using Quora for quite a while but I never wanted to answer questions because I always thought my writing was never good enough.

When I first started I never thought I would end up where I am now.

My initial goal was to improve my writing and nothing more.

I wanted to build a habit that was outside of my comfort zone and I wanted to prove to myself that I could do it.

What started out as a simple challenge became much more.

Through this journey I have been able to meet people from around the world and learn many new ideas from them.

I have learned about everything from the best books to read to what habits the most successful people have.

Quora has allowed me to continue my goal of constant learning and more.

From Quora I have been able to have my writing viewed from all around the world.

I am in the process of having a piece of my writing published.

I have been able to write out my thoughts where thousands of people will be able to read them.

This all started with the simple act of answering one question.

It didn't happen over night. It didn't happen over a couple of months.

It took just a little everyday and that is what I want my readers to understand.

Everywhere I look it seems that everyone is looking to change overnight.

No body wants to put in the work to get the results.

Everyone wants to lose 20 pounds in two weeks or learn a new skill in a couple of days.

Once people do not see results in a couple of days they give up and this mentality is a result of world we live in.

People have seemed to forget that a little everyday can go a long way.

Once I understood that I should focus on making progress everyday rather than just the result everything changed for me.

I became happier that I was accomplishing my daily goals and I was confident I would reach my long term goals later.

This method works for me since I am able to recieve feedback daily it pushes me to keep going.

I can tell by the end of the day whether or not I have done enough for that day.

For example, I started using Duolingo for Chinese for about 15 mintues everday.

Even though it's only 15 mintues a day I have been using this app for a couple of months and I have noticed how much more confident I am in speaking and understanding Chinese.

Once again, I focus on completing the daily goals and the long term goals will eventually be reached.
